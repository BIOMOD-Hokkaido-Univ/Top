Abstract

　Emergence is a phenomenon that indicates complex properties resulting from interactions between smaller or simpler elements which don’t have such properties by themselves.
　For example, a brain which is constructed from nerve cells is a center of learning, memory, action and so on, but individual nerve cells don’t have such complex functions.

　In our study, we use microtubules which act as one of the major cytoskeletons in cells of eukaryotic organisms.
　Microtubules have dynamic structure which conducts polymerization and depolymerization and work with a variety of proteins including the molecular motor. Moreover the structure and dynamics are regulated in the steps of translation and modification.
　As a result, various movements of the cell, such as division and movement, are controlled.

　We focus on the dynamics of microtubules in a cell and try to model a complex emergent phenomenon by using a system which consists of microtubules and molecular motors.

Intro

　A lot of living things including human beings live in a group in the world.
　They have chosen to live in a group as a survival strategy and acquired social nature, such as identifying ability and communication ability. They work not only as individuals, but also as a whole. It is known that there are two ways to let individuals have a function as a group. First one is that the leader determines their strategy and other members follow it (like human beings). This method has been studied well from the point of view of biology, political science and sociology. The other one has become clear as the study of "complex systems" advance. “Emergence” is here.
　Emergence is that a property (or properties) which goes beyond a simple summation of individual properties appears in the absence of a leader. As a result of complexly organized local multiple interactions, a system that cannot be predicted is constructed. Here a character which original individuals don’t have appears.
　Though the term “emergence” is used mainly in the theory of complex systems, it has also been used in various fields and sometimes the interpretation is expanded.
　We can cite ant colony as an example. A queen ant does not give direct orders and tell what to do to the other ants. Instead worker ants react to scent from other ants, intruders, food and waste and then stimulate other ants by leaving new scent. Each worker ant is an autonomous unit that reacts depending only on its local environment and the genetically encoded rules for the variety of ant. Despite the lack of decision making by a leader, ant colony exhibits complex behavior and even has the ability to solve geometric problems. For example, colonies routinely find the maximum distance from all colony entrances to dispose of dead bodies.
　To explore the organisms’ socialization mechanism from the point of view of an emergent phenomenon, we performed a simulation using microtubules and molecular motors which are the constructions in a cell. A molecular motor is a molecular machine that converts chemical energy of ATP to kinetic energy with high efficiency. Myosin moving on actin and kinesin moving on microtubules is a famous example of a molecular motor. A cell can move or divide because these lots of nanoscale molecular motors are working in it.

Design

　When Microtubules are put on a glass plate coated by kinesins, they randomly move on it because of cooperation with kinesins. It is known that microtubules interact each other and move as making rings or bundles when streptavidin are added into microtubules modified by biotins. (//不要かThe reason why this phenomenon happens is strong bonds between streptavidin and biotins.)

　In our research, we observe how microtubules interact each other if there are obstacles made by micro-fabrication technique on the kinesin-coated glass plate. We can analyze emergent phenomena by observing organizational systems which made by coordination of interactions.

Future


　In the future, it is expected that the analysis and successful control of collective actions of microtubules will be used for practical applications both in macro and micro scales.

1.
　By analyzing collective actions of microtubules in different environment, it is possible to simulate how individuals act when they are massed, not just by computers or calculations, but in the real world.
　Microtubules, which are self-propelled micro-molecular proteins, enable this research.
　Since this experiment is done in micro world, it’s easy to use an enormous number of individuals (microtubules) at relatively low cost.
　The results of the experiment can be applied, for example, to predict how people in a building act and try to run away in case of emergency, such as a fire or an earthquake, and help us to devise a building structure or objects in a passage that can induce an efficient evacuation.

2.
　In micro scales, microtubules can be used as transporters of analytes(or some micro materials) when they are on a glass plate whose surface is covered by kinesins.
　This system enables us to concentratae, sort, or detect focused molecules. If patterns of microtubules’ behaviors are analyzed, it is expected that we can improve the precision(or accuracy)of sorting by the difference of the length of microtubules or shapes of obstacles.
　In a medical scene, for instance, this system will be applied to some devises. If it is used as a motor protein-based sorter or concentrator, it will help to detect some specific molecules related deseases from the small amount of blood.
　Thanks to this devises, doctors can diagnose and inspect easily and rapidly.
